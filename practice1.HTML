Editor

Up first are the three poems that make up the Editor collection. Yes, the mistakes in the first two are deliberate...

I

I know the first draft, Is ussually shit, Littered with mistakes, and identity crisis. Can I get the meaning across? Does my black and white, Spell the rainbow?

I make mum read it, The feedback was positive, But not usefull. Need even more help still, To perfect what may become, My best poem yet.

I know I’m always to verbose, I need to cut down the words. Get ready to get my hands dirty. Find the point of the poem, And sharpen it To a razor.

III

  • This must be the third time,
  • I’ve checked over this poem.
  • still searching for grammar,
  • and spelling mistakes.
  • Does my message comute?
  • Do my blacks and whites
  • Paint the rainbows?
  • I need to operate,
  • To salvage what I can.
  • “Give me a scalpel.
  • perhaps the chisel?”
  • Trim the fat,
  • Mend the bone,
  • Feed it protein.
  • I made them read it,
  • My mum, cousin, friend.
  • It’s taking some shape,
  • Eventualy.
  • “Help me to perfect,
  • what may always be imperfect.”

XII

This must be the twelfth time
I’ve read this poem.
There were no spelling mistakes
by draft four.
Does my message transcend?
Do my blacks and whites
Paint the greys?

I became the surgeon,
Put on the apron.
Pass me the scalpel.
Perhaps the bone saw?
Liposuction
Cast
Toning diet

I made them all read it,
My mum,
My cousin,
My friend.
Took it to the workshop.
Help me perfect
This perpetual imperfection.
This poem I’m sick of reading.
